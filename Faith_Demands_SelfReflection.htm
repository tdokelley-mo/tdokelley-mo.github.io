Faith Demands Self-Reflection

God wants us to examine our faith and its progress

Last night was Week 4 in our current Wednesday night men's bible study session. We are walking through a study on developing right thinking and a godly mindset. Our text for the lesson was Romans 12:2. The main ideas were:

1.      Conformity to the world's way of thinking doesn't line up with God's plan for our spiritual development

2.      Renewing of the mind comes with pain, slow progress, and sometimes is difficult, but worth pursuing

3.      God's truth, as presented in the Bible, is the discernment tool to know "that good and acceptable and perfect will of God"

As God would have it, I received my NIV Verse of the Day via email this morning. Guess what it was? Not Romans 12:2, Romans 12:3, the very next verse in that chapter. Paul writes this in Romans 12:3:

 For I say to every man that is among you, through the grace given unto me, not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think, but to think soberly according as God hath dealt to every man the measure of faith. 21st Century King James Version

So, what's Paul getting at here? Well, in the micro-context of verse 2 and 3, Paul is telling us to grow in God's ways and thoughts. Renewing of the mind reveals God's character to us and it builds our faith. It molds our thinking, it guides our actions, and it guards our tongues. That's verse 2. Then, in verse 3, Paul reminds us that God's grace makes all this possible. Paul, having received God's grace, wants to pass along the exhortation against boasting. Too often, we think we've arrived at some enlightenment or some truth because we reasoned it out, we did the work, we, we, we. You know what I mean!

Paul says the opposite. The word used for "highly" in verse 3 means literally "to think more highly of one's self than is proper". That sounds like excess, to me. Or extreme, we can go to extremes demonstrating how well we are doing in our faith journey. Contrast that with Paul's advice to think soberly. It translates as being in one's right mind, exercising self-control, and putting a moderate estimate on yourself. Paul reminds us that we need to reflect constantly on where we are spiritually. How are we doing with, "fill in the blank". It could be a sin issue, a relationship issue, a pride issue, or even a biblical understanding issue. The key point is we are hard at work! We are thinking rightly, and we are growing! Paul sums up this verse with another reminder. Ultimately, God measures our faith. God has given us a measure to work with for a time. We partner with the Holy Spirit and consume that measure. When we do, God will measure out again, and again, and again, until we are complete! Not here, but one day, in His presence.

Here's some action steps:

1.      Don't be afraid to reflect on your faith. Has it changed? Is that change good progress or is it negative? If negative, find some wise brethren and seek their counsel!

2.      Remember to partner with the Holy Spirit. Reflect on the measure of faith given. Where is it weak? Where is it strong? Pray to God to increase what is weak and make the strong even stronger.

3.      Take a long look at your attitude regarding your faith. Are you boastful, are you excessive? Pray God will help you examine your spiritual journey and your growing faith in a humble spirit of partnership with God.

Thanks for reading.
